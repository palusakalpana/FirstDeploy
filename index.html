About the Author

Haemin Sunim, whose name means "Spontaneous Wisdom," is a renowned Korean Buddhist monk, teacher, and author. Born in 1973, he pursued his education in the United States, studying at Harvard and Princeton before embracing monastic life. Haemin’s teachings focus on integrating Buddhist philosophy with modern psychology, offering practical advice on mindfulness, compassion, and inner peace. He is celebrated for his accessible writing style and ability to convey profound truths simply.

Overview

This internationally acclaimed book explores the transformative power of mindfulness and how slowing down in life can lead to inner peace. Written by Haemin Sunim, the book combines Buddhist-inspired philosophy, practical advice, and life lessons with a focus on handling relationships, work, and daily challenges. Each chapter is accompanied by serene illustrations that amplify the book’s calming essence.


With its straightforward language, the book delves into universal aspects of life, such as love, loss, hope, and healing. It resonates with readers across cultures, offering a gentle guide to achieving tranquility in a fast-paced world.

Structure of the Book

The book is divided into eight chapters, each centered on a specific theme:

  1. Rest:The necessity of slowing down and taking mindful breaks.
  2. Mindfulness:The art of being present and appreciating the moment.
  3. Passion:Finding balance between enthusiasm and peace.
  4. Relationships:Building and nurturing meaningful connections.
  5. Love:Fostering self-love and unconditional love for others.
  6. Life:Embracing life’s uncertainties and challenges with grace.
  7. The Future:Approaching the unknown with faith and courage.
  8. Spirituality:Cultivating inner wisdom and compassion for spiritual growth.
